---

BlueRetro is open-source firmware for the ESP32 that lets you use modern Bluetooth controllers — PS3, PS4, PS5, Xbox One, Xbox Series X|S, Nintendo Switch, Wii, and generic HID devices — on retro game consoles and computers. A single adapter supports up to 4+ simultaneous players.

## Get started in three steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get the hardware">
    Pick up an ESP32 development board (ESP32-DevKitC recommended) and wire it to your console's controller port. See the [hardware guide](/hardware) for pinouts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Flash the firmware">
    Download the latest BlueRetro firmware release and flash it to your ESP32 using the web flasher or the `flash_blueretro.py` script. See the [quick start guide](/quickstart).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pair your controller and play">
    Put your Bluetooth controller in pairing mode. BlueRetro discovers it automatically. Open the web config interface to customize button mapping and settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  BlueRetro is open-source hardware and firmware released under the CERN-OHL-P-2.0 and Apache-2.0 licenses. Community-made PCBs and pre-built adapters are available from makers like [Laser Bear Industries](https://www.laserbear.net), [Humble Bazooka](https://www.humblebazooka.com), [RetroOnyx](https://www.retroonyx.com/), and [RetroTime](https://8bitmods.com/retrotime).
</Note>
